Inventory KPIs

KPI Definition
Inventory Turnover (ann.) Measures how many times the total inventory is sold and replenished within a year. A higher rate indicates stronger sales velocity relative to stock held.
Days Sales of Inventory The number of days of stock on hand at the current rate of sales. A lower figure means inventory converts to cash faster. High DSI indicates capital is tied up in stock.
GMROI Gross Margin Return on Inventory Investment. Measures the gross profit generated per rand of average inventory held. A ratio above 1.0x means inventory earns more than it costs to hold.
Stock-to-Sales Ratio Closing inventory divided by monthly revenue. Indicates how much stock is being held relative to what is being sold. A declining ratio over time signals improving efficiency.
Avg COGS per Box Sold Total cost of goods sold divided by total boxes sold for the month. Tracks the average cost to produce and deliver one box.

Cash Position

LIQUIDITY
January's R215k peak reflects receipt of the large Arcadia reseller payment. February's closing cash of R147k reflects subsequent inventory purchases. Cash coverage relative to monthly operating expenses remained above two months throughout the FY26 period. An assumption of 110 boxes per month given away for marketing purposes has been applied.

Inventory Position

STOCK
Total inventory increased from R198k (February) to R487k (March), an increase of R289k. This is driven by a large electrolyte powder purchase (Inventory Electrolytes increased from R22k to R230k per the Balance Sheet). This represents a deliberate forward purchase to support anticipated FY2027 demand growth, and will temporarily elevate DSI and the stock-to-sales ratio until the stock is converted into product and sold.
